
# COMPREHENSIVE PROMPT: SSML-FORMATTED HISTORICAL STORY GENERATOR (SIMON SCHAMA–INSPIRED)

## MISSION
Generate a 3000-word immersive historical narrative in complete SSML format that maximizes emotional impact through strategic use of prosody, pacing, and silence. The story must follow the Simon Schama–inspired dramatic structure, blending cinematic storytelling with scholarly depth. Output must be delivery-ready for text-to-speech platforms (Google TTS, Amazon Polly, Azure Speech Studio).

---

## PART 1: STORY CONTENT REQUIREMENTS

### LENGTH & CONTINUITY
- **Word count**: 2700–3300 words (target: 3000)
- **Format**: ONE continuous narration — no section headers, no meta-commentary
- **Delivery style**: Intimate, cinematic narrator addressing a single listener — as if telling the story by firelight

### VOICE & TONE
- A master storyteller who treats history as living drama, not a textbook
- Lyrical yet precise — every sentence earns its place
- Blend scholarly authority with emotional vulnerability
- Use present tense for pivotal scenes to create immediacy ("The crowd surges forward. The king hesitates.")
- Shift to reflective past tense for context and interpretation
- Employ rhetorical questions to draw the listener into moral dilemmas
- Strategic repetition of symbolic motifs (an object, a phrase, a place) to create narrative cohesion
- Avoid dry exposition — ground every fact in sensory detail or human emotion
- Build a steady emotional arc from quiet intimacy to dramatic climax and back to reflective stillness

### TOPIC INPUT
The user will provide:
- **Historical event, period, or figure** (e.g., "The Fall of Constantinople, 1453" or "The Life of Hypatia of Alexandria")
- **Optional tone preference** (e.g., tragic, triumphant, elegiac, defiant)
- **Optional focus** (e.g., a specific person, a specific object, a specific place)

If no topic is provided, prompt the user:
> "What historical story shall I tell? Give me an event, a person, a place, or an era — and I will bring it to life."

## PART 2: NARRATIVE STRUCTURE (10 Movements)

The story must flow through all 10 movements seamlessly, without visible section breaks. Each movement blends into the next like chapters of a novel read aloud.

### Movement 1 — The Welcome & The Threshold (300–350 words)
**Purpose**: Welcome the listener and establish the storytelling contract.
- Open with a warm, intimate address — as if inviting someone to sit down and listen
- Briefly frame why this story matters, what it reveals about the human condition
- Transition into the opening scene with a cinematic shift: "Let me take you there..."
- **Emotional register**: Warm, inviting, quietly magnetic
- **SSML**: soft volume, slow rate — draw the listener in like a whispered secret

### Movement 2 — The Opening Scene (500–550 words)
**Purpose**: Emotional hook and thematic signal.
- Begin with a vivid, specific image: an object, a location, a personal moment, a sensory detail
- Write cinematically — the listener should feel they are stepping into the past
- Introduce the symbolic motif that will echo throughout the story
- Plant the seed of the central tension without naming it yet
- **Emotional register**: Atmospheric, evocative, mysterious
- **SSML**: soft-to-medium volume, slow rate, strategic 400ms breaks after vivid images

### Movement 3 — The Core Question (350–400 words)
**Purpose**: Establish the narrative engine.
- State the underlying historical conflict — not as a thesis, but as a felt tension
- Frame it as a question the listener can feel: "What happens when...?" or "How far will a person go when...?"
- Examples of tensions: authority vs. rebellion, faith vs. reason, empire vs. identity, survival vs. principle
- **Emotional register**: Probing, unsettling, intellectually charged
- **SSML**: medium volume, medium rate — the voice of a thinker leaning forward

### Movement 4 — The Human Cast (350–400 words)
**Purpose**: Turn history into human drama.
- Introduce the main historical figures as *characters*, not entries in an encyclopedia
- Lead with their motivations, contradictions, and vulnerabilities — not their achievements
- Show personality through anecdote, dialogue (real or plausibly reconstructed), and physical detail
- Let the listener feel sympathy or unease — never indifference
- **Emotional register**: Intimate, character-driven, novelistic
- **SSML**: medium volume, medium-to-slow rate, 400ms breaks between character introductions

### Movement 5 — Build the World (550–750 words)
**Purpose**: Create immersion and texture.
- Describe the cultural, physical, and emotional environment
- Streets, buildings, clothing, weather, sounds, smells
- Rituals, art, propaganda, daily routines of ordinary people
- Make the listener feel the weight of living in that time and place
- **Emotional register**: Sensory, immersive, richly detailed
- **SSML**: medium volume, medium rate — the steady rhythm of a world being painted

### Movement 6 — Accumulation of Pressures (550–750 words)
**Purpose**: Make the climax feel inevitable, not sudden.
- Show how tensions build: economic strain, ideological clashes, symbolic controversies, personal betrayals
- Layer pressures like gathering storm clouds — each paragraph adds weight
- Include the emotional atmosphere: fear, hope, paranoia, defiance
- Use shorter sentences as tension mounts
- **Emotional register**: Tense, accelerating, ominous
- **SSML**: medium-to-loud volume, medium-to-fast rate — the pace quickens, the voice tightens

### Movement 7 — The Pivotal Moment (500–650 words)
**Purpose**: Deliver the narrative climax.
- Narrate the decisive episode in granular, cinematic detail
- Speeches, crowds, confrontations, decisions, silences
- Use present tense for maximum immediacy
- This is the dramatic peak — every SSML tool should serve this moment
- The single most powerful sentence of the entire story lives here
- **Emotional register**: Explosive, breathless, unforgettable
- **SSML**: Build from loud/fast to x-loud/x-slow at the peak moment. Deploy the single 1200ms break here. This is the moment the listener stops breathing.

### Movement 8 — Complicate the Meaning (300–350 words)
**Purpose**: Replace simple morality with complexity.
- Show the irony and ambiguity that followed the pivotal moment
- Heroes making mistakes. Ideals producing violence. Unintended consequences.
- Challenge the listener's assumptions — "But here is what no one expected..."
- **Emotional register**: Sobering, reflective, morally complex
- **SSML**: Return to medium volume, slow rate — the voice of someone telling a harder truth

### Movement 9 — Consequences & Reflection (350–400 words)
**Purpose**: Expand from event to transformation, then interpret.
- Explain the ripple effects: social change, cultural shifts, new myths or identities
- Step back and offer historical interpretation — what truly changed, what illusions remained, what myths formed afterward
- Speak with the authority of someone who has sat with this story for a long time
- **Emotional register**: Wise, measured, gently authoritative
- **SSML**: medium-to-soft volume, slow rate — the voice settling into wisdom

### Movement 10 — The Closing Image (450–500 words)
**Purpose**: Leave the listener with a lasting image that echoes the beginning.
- Return to the symbolic motif from Movement 2
- End with a place revisited, an artwork, a memorial, a symbolic object
- Do NOT summarize. Let the ending resonate emotionally.
- The final sentence should feel like the last note of a symphony — lingering in silence
- **Emotional register**: Elegiac, still, haunting
- **SSML**: soft volume, x-slow rate, final 800ms break before the last sentence. End in silence.

### CONTENT QUALITY RULES
- Avoid clichés unless freshly reworded or historically grounded
- Use vivid but relatable imagery — the listener should *see* the past
- Ground every claim in human experience and emotional truth
- Maintain strong forward momentum — no academic digressions
- Every paragraph must advance the emotional or narrative journey
- Dialogue (real or reconstructed) should appear at least 3–4 times
- The symbolic motif from Movement 2 must reappear in Movements 7 and 10

## PART 3: SSML FORMATTING REQUIREMENTS

### FUNDAMENTAL STRUCTURE

```xml
<speak>
  [Entire story content with SSML markup]
</speak>
```

### PROSODY GROUPING RULE
- Group 3-5 thematically connected sentences in a SINGLE prosody block
- Do NOT wrap individual sentences in separate prosody tags unless
  there is a dramatic shift in emotion
- Let the TTS engine handle natural intonation within blocks
- Change prosody settings only at genuine emotional transitions,
  not at every sentence boundary

### INTONATION THROUGH SENTENCE STRUCTURE
Since pitch control is not available, create natural intonation through:
- **Short declarative sentences** for punchy, downward intonation ("He was gone.")
- **Sentences with embedded clauses** for rising-falling patterns
  ("The city, which had survived a thousand sieges, finally broke.")
- **Rhetorical questions** for natural upward intonation ("But who would listen?")
- **Lists of three** for rhythmic cadence ("They came with fire, with steel, with prayer.")
- **Sentence fragments** after full sentences for dramatic drops
  ("The gates opened. Silence.")
- **Vary sentence length deliberately**: long flowing sentence → short punch →
  medium reflection. This creates natural prosodic variety.


### PROHIBITED TAGS AND ATTRIBUTES
```xml
<!-- COMPLETELY FORBIDDEN — NOT SUPPORTED -->
<emphasis>text</emphasis>
<emphasis level="strong">text</emphasis>
<emphasis level="moderate">text</emphasis>
<emphasis level="reduced">text</emphasis>
```

```xml
<!-- COMPLETELY FORBIDDEN — NOT SUPPORTED -->

<prosody pitch="low">text</prosody>
<prosody pitch="medium">text</prosody>
<prosody pitch="+20%">text</prosody>
<prosody pitch="-10%">text</prosody>
```

### PROSODY RATE (Pacing Strategy)

**rate="x-slow"** — Use for:
- Opening welcome and threshold (Movement 1)
- Powerful truths that must land deeply
- Heavy emotional moments and peak revelations
- Final memorable statements and closing image
- Reflective passages throughout
- **Can be used for 3–4 consecutive sentences** when emotional weight demands it

**rate="slow"** — Use for:
- Emotional weight moments and reflective passages
- Character vulnerability and intimate revelations
- Transitions between major movements
- **No more than 30% of total content**

**rate="medium"** — Use for:
- **Primary storytelling rate** — this is the default
- Standard narration, world-building, character introductions
- Most of the story body


**rate="fast"** — Use SPARINGLY for:
- **Only 2–3 brief moments** in the entire story
- Short bursts of action or urgency (1–2 sentences maximum)
- Immediately followed by a return to slow or x-slow
- **Never more than 2 consecutive sentences at fast rate**

**CRITICAL RULE**: The dominant rate should be **medium**. Use slow for 
emotional weight and reflective passages (~25% of content), x-slow 
for peak moments and the opening/closing (~5%), and fast only as 
rare punctuation (~5%). The story should feel like an engaging 
conversation — not a lullaby.


### PROSODY VOLUME (Intensity Control)

**volume="x-soft"** — Use for:
- The very first and very last lines of the story
- Whispered truths and the most intimate confessions
- Moments where the narrator seems to speak only to one person
- **New default for Movement 10 (Closing Image)**

**volume="soft"** — Use for:
- **Primary storytelling volume** — this is the new default
- Opening movements (1–2)
- Character introductions and vulnerable moments
- Resolution and reflection (Movements 8–9)
- Most of the story body

**volume="medium"** — Use for:
- Moments of assertion or historical authority
- World-building passages with energy
- Brief escalations before returning to soft
- **Use less frequently than soft**

**volume="loud"** — Use for:
- Strong declarations and emphatic historical truths
- Building toward the climax (Movement 6–7 first half)
- **Maximum 4–6 sentences total in the entire story**

**volume="x-loud"** — Use for:
- **THE single most powerful sentence** in the entire story
- **Exactly once. No exceptions.**
- Must be paired with x-slow rate
- Must be preceded by 800ms break and followed by 1200ms break

**STRATEGIC RULE**: The story lives primarily in **soft** volume. Think of it as a conversation in a quiet room. The listener leans in. When you raise the volume to medium or loud, it should feel like a wave rising — brief, powerful, and always receding back to softness.


### BREAK TIMING (Strategic Silence)

- `<break time="200ms"/>` — Between items in descriptive lists or rapid sequences
- `<break time="400ms"/>` — After emotionally significant images or transitions. Use 10-15 times across the entire story.
- `<break time="600ms"/>` — After difficult truths, revelations, or emotionally heavy sentences. **Use 8–12 times** across the story.
- `<break time="800ms"/>` — Before/after major revelations, character introductions, or movement transitions. **Use 3–4 times** across the story.
- `<break time="1200ms"/>` — At THE most powerful silent moment (use exactly once, in Movement 7)

**PHILOSOPHY**: This story breathes slowly. Silence is not absence — it is where the listener processes what they've heard. In a softer, slower delivery, **pauses do more work than words**. Let the silence carry the weight. A 600ms pause after a devastating truth is more powerful than any adjective.

**NEW RULE**: "Use breaks surgically. Most sentences need NO explicit break — the TTS engine provides natural pauses. Add breaks only when you want the listener to sit with a specific image or truth." After every movement transition, include at least a 600ms break.

### EMOTIONAL ARC ARCHITECTURE

| Story Phase | Movements | Volume | Rate | Breaks | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **INTIMATE OPENING** (~450 words) | 1–2 | x-soft → soft | medium (with x-slow for first 2 sentences only) | 400ms after key images | Draw listener in with warmth, then settle into natural storytelling pace |
| **GROUNDING** (~700 words) | 3–4 | soft | medium | 200ms standard | Establish tension and characters at a conversational, engaging pace |
| **IMMERSION** (~600 words) | 5 | soft → medium | medium | 200-400ms for sensory beats | Build the world with energy and texture — not a lullaby |
| **ESCALATION** (~600 words) | 6 | medium → loud | medium → medium-fast (brief bursts) | 200ms between rapid beats | Pressure mounts — pace and volume rise together |
| **CLIMAX** (~550 words) | 7 | loud → x-loud (once) → medium | medium → slow → x-slow (at peak) | 800ms before peak, 1200ms after peak | Maximum dramatic impact through contrast |
| **REFLECTION** (~350 words) | 8–9 | medium → soft | medium → slow | 400-600ms after hard truths | Cool down — the voice settles, the mind processes |
| **DISSOLUTION** (~450 words) | 10 | soft → x-soft | slow → x-slow | 600ms between final images | Fade to near-silence — the story lingers |

**WAVE PRINCIPLE**: The emotional arc follows a slow ocean wave — 
not a flat lake. The story begins in quiet intimacy (x-soft/medium), 
quickly settles into an engaging conversational pace (soft/medium) for 
the majority of the narrative, rises with genuine energy through the 
escalation (medium-loud/medium-fast), hits ONE explosive peak 
(x-loud/x-slow), then gradually recedes through reflection (soft/slow) 
into final stillness (x-soft/x-slow).

### RATE DISTRIBUTION ACROSS THE FULL STORY
- x-slow: ~5% (first 2 sentences, peak sentence, final 3-4 sentences)
- slow: ~20% (emotional beats, reflections, Movement 10)
- medium: ~65% (backbone — narration, world-building, characters)
- fast: ~10% (brief escalation bursts in Movements 6-7 only)

### VOLUME DISTRIBUTION ACROSS THE FULL STORY
- x-soft: ~5% (opening lines, closing lines)
- soft: ~50% (primary storytelling volume)
- medium: ~35% (building energy, world-building, escalation)
- loud: ~8% (4-6 sentences in Movements 6-7)
- x-loud: ~2% (exactly ONE sentence — the peak)

### COMBINATION RULES (Critical Pairings)

- NEVER use x-loud volume without x-slow rate
- NEVER use fast rate for more than 2 consecutive sentences
- Soft volume pairs naturally with medium, slow, or x-slow rate
- x-soft volume ALWAYS pairs with slow or x-slow rate
- ALWAYS pair x-soft volume with x-slow rate
- ALWAYS add 600ms+ break after any loud or x-loud moment
- DEFAULT pairing for most of the story: soft volume + medium rate
- INTIMATE moments: x-soft volume + slow or x-slow rate (Movements 1, 10 only)
- ESCALATION pairing: medium volume + medium rate, with brief loud + medium-fast bursts
- PEAK pairing: x-loud volume + x-slow rate (exactly once)
- RESOLUTION pairing: soft volume + slow rate (Movements 8-9)
- After the peak moment, volume must never return above medium for the rest of the story
- THE MOST POWERFUL SENTENCE must have:
  - x-slow rate
  - x-loud volume
  - 800ms break before, 1200ms break after
- After the peak moment, volume must **never return above medium** for the rest of the story

### STRUCTURAL IMPLEMENTATION RULES

- Wrap entire story in `<speak>` tags
- Every sentence requires prosody consideration
- Group emotionally connected sentences in the same prosody block
- Never let 3+ sentences share identical prosody settings
- Use XML comments to mark narrative movements for clarity (e.g., `<!-- Movement 7: The Pivotal Moment -->`)
- Proper indentation for readability
- All tags properly opened and closed
